What do I need to know before I start?

The Auslan sign bank uses a variety of web technolgies, such as: cookies, Macromedia Flash and Javascript. Please read the System Requirements below and make sure that you are using a compatible web browser and you have the required version of Macromedia Flash.

What browser should I use?

The Auslan Signbank site has been tested and confirmed to work with the following web browsers:

  • Microsoft Internet Explorer 5.0 and greater (Windows and Mac OSX)
  • Netscape 7.1 and greater (Windows)
  • Mozilla Firefox (Windows and Mac OSX)
  • Safari (Mac OSX)

The site may work on other browsers - but we do not guarantee compatibility. If you are experiencing problems and you do not have one of the web browsers above, we recommend that you download Mozilla Firefox from www.mozilla.org.

What version of Flash do I require?

In order to view sign "videos" on the site, you will need to have the Macromedia Flash Player plug-in installed on your computer. The videos require Flash Player 7 or greater. To verify what version of Flash is installed with your browser, you can go to www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer.

What screen resolution works best?

The Auslan Signbank displays best on screen resolutions of 800 by 600 or higher. Please consider increasing your screen resolution if it is lower than 800x600 so that our content will fit within the width of your screen.

What can I do?

  • Search for a sign by typing in an English word
  • view and practice fingerspelling
  • view number signs
  • give feedback on existing signs
  • report missing signs
  • view the feedback of various groups of people

Feedback viewing according to groups is only possible if more than certain number of responses have been received about a particular sign, otherwise percentages are misleading.

How do I navigate around the site?

You click on the tabs at the top of this page for each of the main functions. You can also click on and button when you are searching for a sign if there is more than one sign that matches the English word. You can provide feedback about any sign using the link below the sign video. If other users have provided feedback on a sign there will also be a link to allow you to view a summary of this feedback.

Why is there no matching sign for my English word? 

  • Did you spell the word correctly? Check your spelling. Hint: use the simple form of a word when looking for a sign (e.g., 'car' not 'cars'; 'walk' not 'walked'; 'go' not 'went').
  • The word might not be in Signbank for the sign you are thinking of. Try another word with a similar meaning (e.g, try 'crazy' instead of 'lunatic'). Hint: if you think a word should be added to the meaning of a sign select 'Provide feedback about this sign' and tell Signbank.
  • The sign might be missing from Signbank. Use the 'feedback' tab to report the missing sign.
  • The sign might be a new sign that only you or a small group of people know or use. Use the 'feedback' tab to tell Signbank, and everyone else, about your new sign.

Why does Signbank sometimes give me the 'wrong' sign for my word?

Maybe there is more than one Auslan sign for the word you typed in. If appears above the sign definition that means there are more Auslan signs to view for that word. Click on the button to see the other sign or signs. If you think there is really a mistake, click on 'Provide feedback about this sign' and tell Signbank.

Why do I need to register?

You need to register to provide feedback or report missing signs. We need you to register in order to know who you are (e.g., a deaf person or a hearing person) and where you come from (e.g., Australia or overseas) so we can group your feedback. That way when you view user feedback on Signbank you will be able to clearly see the opinions of Australian deaf people compared to other groups of people. After all, it is our language. Our opinion matters!

How do I register?

You only need to register for Signbank if you want to provide feedback on a sign or the site in general, or provide details of a missing sign. When you click on a feedback link, you will be prompted to login or register a new account.

How do I leave a video comment?

You can upload a short video in each of the feedback forms if you would rather sign your comment instead of typing or filling out the form. This will be particularly useful if you are telling us about a missing sign. To upload a video, first record your video on your own computer, save it in a common format (eg. AVI on Windows or Quicktime on Macintosh) and upload it using the special field in the feedback form. Your videos will be made available to the site administrators but will not be viewable by the general public.

Any movie recording software can be used to record your video. For example, iCamShare for the Macintosh provides a simple interface. On Windows you can often use the tools that are provided with your webcam, eg. Logitech provide a set of programs for recording videos etc.

Does Signbank use Cookies?

Your web browser must be configured to accept cookies from the Auslan site to allow you to login and provide comments. The cookie identifies you to the site once you are logged in and is deleted once you close your browser.
